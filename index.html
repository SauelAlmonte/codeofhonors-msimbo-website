Msimbo means "Codes" in Swahili. A twenty weeks program for displaced, unemployed and underemployed workers interested becoming computer programmers.

This program seek to address the IT industries' issue of diversity and skilled workers. ULEM looks to enroll 18 students and provide a stipend for $100/ week.

MSIMBO bootcamp trains talented, low-income individuals to become full-stack web developers and launch a new career students are trained in web development, effective business communication and leadership.

We will conduct a 20 weeks of 120 hours coding technology training program and the professional development for ULEM's MSIMBO program.
